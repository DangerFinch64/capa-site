What CAPA does

Give your dispute the structure it deserves.

Whether you are dealing with a service failure, a billing dispute, an unresolved complaint, or an organisation that has not met its obligations — CAPA gives you the tools to pursue it properly.

  • Know your best path Understand what policies, regulations, and escalation options apply to your situation before you act. CAPA maps the landscape so you can choose the highest-leverage route.
  • Build a stronger evidence file Bring together facts, correspondence, timelines, and supporting documents in one structured place. A well-organised evidence file is the foundation of any effective dispute.
  • Draft smarter complaints Communications that are clear, policy-referenced, and professionally structured carry more weight. CAPA helps you say the right things in the right way.
  • Escalate to the right channel Know when and how to escalate — to internal complaints handlers, regulators, ombudsmen, or consumer tribunals. CAPA identifies the right channel for your situation.
  • Keep control of what gets shared You decide what information is disclosed and when. CAPA operates on minimum-necessary-disclosure principles — nothing is shared without your explicit direction.

Why CAPA

Not a chatbot. Not a law firm. Something more useful.

Most dispute tools are either generic chat interfaces or expensive legal services. CAPA is neither — it is a structured dispute-management platform built specifically for consumer cases.

  • Structured, not conversational CAPA organises your case systematically — timeline, evidence, policy references, escalation options — not through an open-ended chat that loses context.
  • Evidence-backed, not guess-based Disputes are won on evidence and policy, not sentiment. CAPA helps you build a file that can withstand scrutiny and support formal escalation.
  • Policy-aware, not generic CAPA identifies the regulations, codes of practice, and dispute schemes that apply to your specific situation — not boilerplate advice that fits every case equally badly.
  • User-controlled, not automated Every disclosure, every communication, every escalation step is yours to approve. CAPA does not act on your behalf without explicit instruction and review.
  • Escalation-ready, not just advisory CAPA is built to move cases forward — to regulators, ombudsmen, and consumer tribunals. Knowing where to go is the starting point, not the finish line.

Privacy & control

Your case. Your information. Your call.

Dispute support requires sharing sensitive information — correspondence, account details, case documents. CAPA is built around the principle that you remain in control of every disclosure.

  • Only what you share CAPA collects and retains only the information needed for your stated purpose — nothing more, nothing aggregated beyond what you explicitly agreed to.
  • Nothing sent without your say-so Where CAPA acts on your behalf — sending communications, sharing evidence — it does so only within the authority you explicitly grant. You review before anything is sent.
  • Evidence protection Your case documents, correspondence, and evidence are handled securely and are not shared with third parties without your direction.

Common questions

The questions people usually ask first

Is CAPA a law firm or legal service?

No. CAPA Resolve is not a law firm and does not provide legal advice. It is a case-management and dispute-support platform. Using CAPA does not create a solicitor-client or attorney-client relationship. If your situation requires legal representation, you should consult a qualified solicitor or attorney.

What kinds of disputes does CAPA handle?

CAPA is built for consumer disputes: subscription and billing complaints, telecom and internet service disputes, travel disruptions, and general service failures with businesses. CAPA is not designed for active litigation, criminal matters, or disputes that require formal legal representation.

Does CAPA contact companies on my behalf?

Only with your explicit instruction. CAPA can help you draft communications and, where authorised, assist with sending them — but nothing is sent without your review and approval. You stay in control of every disclosure.

Does CAPA guarantee an outcome?

No. Disputes are inherently uncertain and outcomes depend on many factors outside any platform's control. CAPA helps you be better prepared, better organised, and better positioned — but we do not make outcome guarantees.
