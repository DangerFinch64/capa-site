Cookies Policy

Last updated: April 2026

What are cookies?

Cookies are small text files that websites can store in your browser to remember information about your visit. They are used for a wide range of purposes, from keeping you signed in to a service to tracking how visitors use a website.

Does this website use cookies?

The current version of the CAPA Resolve website is a minimal static site. We do not currently use advertising, analytics, or tracking cookies. We do not use any third-party tracking scripts, social media pixels, or fingerprinting techniques.

Your hosting provider (Cloudflare) may set certain technical cookies as part of delivering the website securely and efficiently. These are infrastructure-level cookies related to security (such as bot detection) and are not used by CAPA Resolve to track individuals or serve advertising.

Future changes

As CAPA Resolve launches its platform and adds functionality, our use of cookies may change. For example, we may add:

  • Session cookies to keep users authenticated in the platform
  • Preference cookies to remember display settings
  • Analytics tools to understand how the website or platform is being used (in which case, we would describe these here and, where required by law, obtain your consent)

We will update this policy before making any such changes that would affect your experience or require your consent.

Managing cookies

Most browsers allow you to control cookie settings through their preferences. You can typically block all cookies, delete existing cookies, or set your browser to notify you when a cookie is being set. Note that blocking all cookies may affect the functionality of some websites.

For more information on managing cookies, visit your browser's help documentation or a resource such as aboutcookies.org.

Questions

If you have questions about how this website uses cookies, contact us at privacy@caparesolve.com. See also the Privacy Policy for broader information on how we handle personal information.
