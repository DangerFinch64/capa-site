Subprocessors

Third-party providers that may process data in connection with CAPA Resolve services.

Last updated: April 2026

This page lists the categories of third-party providers CAPA Resolve uses or expects to use. The specific providers and the scope of their data processing will be confirmed and updated as the platform moves into production. The categories below reflect the intended architecture; they do not represent a confirmed live list of all active providers.

Overview

Like most modern web services, CAPA Resolve relies on a small number of trusted third-party providers to deliver its infrastructure, communications, and operational capabilities. These providers may process personal data on our behalf as "subprocessors" within the meaning of applicable data protection law.

We engage subprocessors carefully, require them to handle data in accordance with applicable law and our instructions, and ensure appropriate contractual protections are in place, including data processing agreements where required.

Specific provider names

We will publish specific provider names here once the production platform is configured and providers are confirmed. We believe transparency requires accuracy, and listing provider names before they are confirmed is more likely to mislead than to inform.

Customers with enterprise or contractual requirements for a full, named subprocessor list should contact legal@caparesolve.com.

Updates to this list

We will update this page as the platform evolves and new subprocessors are engaged or existing ones are changed. Customers who have executed Data Processing Agreements may have notification rights regarding subprocessor changes; please refer to your agreement for details.
